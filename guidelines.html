General Conduct

Expected Behavior

  • Treat all community members with respect and dignity
  • Engage in constructive and meaningful discussions
  • Respect diverse perspectives and cultural backgrounds
  • Use appropriate language free from hate speech or discrimination
  • Report inappropriate content or behavior to administrators

Prohibited Activities

  • Harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any kind
  • Discriminatory language or behavior based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, or cultural identity
  • Spam, self-promotion, or commercial activities
  • Sharing false or misleading information
  • Attempting to circumvent moderation decisions

Submission Guidelines

Before You Submit

Important: Only submit artwork that you have permission to share publicly. This includes obtaining proper consent from artists, cultural authorities, or landowners.

What You Can Submit

  • Indigenous art in public spaces (with proper permissions)
  • Art in museums or galleries (with institution consent)
  • Your own indigenous artwork
  • Historical pieces with proper documentation
  • Community-approved cultural expressions

What You Cannot Submit

  • Sacred or ceremonial objects without explicit community approval
  • Artwork from private collections without owner consent
  • Images taken on private property without permission
  • Culturally sensitive items that should remain private
  • Reproductions or replicas presented as originals
  • Copyright-protected contemporary works without permission

Required Information

  • Accurate title and description
  • Correct cultural attribution when known
  • Appropriate time period classification
  • Location information (respecting sensitivity requirements)
  • Source of permission to share the artwork

Cultural Sensitivity

Respectful Representation

  • Use appropriate terminology when describing indigenous cultures
  • Avoid generalizations or stereotypes
  • Credit artists and communities accurately when known
  • Respect cultural protocols around sacred or sensitive content
  • Acknowledge when information is incomplete or uncertain

Sacred and Sensitive Content

Special Considerations

Some indigenous art may have religious, ceremonial, or cultural significance that requires special handling. When in doubt, consult with relevant cultural authorities before submitting.

Community Consultation

  • Engage with relevant indigenous communities when possible
  • Respect community decisions about what should or should not be shared
  • Understand that some art may be meant for specific audiences only
  • Be open to feedback and corrections from community members

Image Requirements

Technical Specifications

  • Format: JPEG or PNG files only
  • Size: Maximum 10MB per image
  • Resolution: Minimum 800x600 pixels recommended
  • Quality: Clear, well-lit, and in focus
  • Quantity: Multiple angles encouraged when appropriate

Photography Guidelines

  • Obtain permission before photographing artwork
  • Respect any photography restrictions at the location
  • Avoid flash photography that could damage artwork
  • Include context shots when helpful for understanding
  • Ensure images accurately represent colors and details

Privacy Considerations

  • Avoid including people in artwork photographs without consent
  • Remove or blur any personal information visible in images
  • Be mindful of location identifying features if anonymity is needed

Location Guidelines

Location Sensitivity

Public Locations

Artwork in public spaces, museums, or galleries can generally include specific location information.

Sensitive Locations

Sacred sites, private land, or culturally sensitive areas should use general region information only.

Protected Locations

Some locations may need to remain completely anonymous for protection and preservation.

When to Mark as Sensitive

  • Sacred or ceremonial sites
  • Private property or restricted areas
  • Locations vulnerable to vandalism or damage
  • Sites with ongoing cultural or legal disputes
  • Areas where indigenous communities have requested privacy

Location Description Best Practices

  • Provide helpful context without compromising sensitivity
  • Use landmarks or general areas when specific coordinates are inappropriate
  • Include access information when publicly available
  • Note any restrictions or special requirements for visiting

Moderation Process

Review Process

  1. Submission: All submissions are reviewed before becoming public
  2. Initial Review: Basic compliance check for guidelines and technical requirements
  3. Cultural Review: Cultural sensitivity and appropriateness assessment
  4. Decision: Approve, request modifications, or reject with explanation
  5. Publication: Approved submissions become publicly visible

Review Timeline

  • Most submissions reviewed within 3-5 business days
  • Complex or sensitive submissions may require additional time
  • Submitters will be notified of the decision via email
  • Appeals process available for rejected submissions

Moderation Criteria

  • Compliance with usage guidelines
  • Cultural sensitivity and appropriateness
  • Accuracy of information provided
  • Image quality and relevance
  • Proper permissions and attributions

Community Standards

Building a Respectful Community

  • Welcome newcomers and help them understand our guidelines
  • Provide constructive feedback on submissions when appropriate
  • Share knowledge respectfully and cite sources
  • Engage in meaningful discussions about art and culture
  • Report violations of guidelines to moderators

Intellectual Property Respect

  • Respect copyright and cultural intellectual property rights
  • Provide proper attribution to artists and communities
  • Understand that some knowledge may be considered proprietary
  • Avoid unauthorized reproduction or commercial use of submitted content

Educational Purpose

  • Focus on educational and cultural preservation goals
  • Share context and historical information when known
  • Correct misinformation respectfully and provide sources
  • Encourage learning about indigenous cultures and histories

Consequences

Violation Response

Minor Violations

Warning, guidance, and opportunity to correct the issue

Moderate Violations

Temporary restriction of submission privileges or account suspension

Severe Violations

Permanent account suspension or ban from the platform

Appeals Process

  • Users may appeal moderation decisions through our contact form
  • Appeals will be reviewed by a different moderator when possible
  • Additional context or evidence may be considered
  • Final decisions will be communicated within 7 business days

Content Removal

  • Content that violates guidelines will be removed
  • Users will be notified of removals with explanations
  • Opportunities for revision and resubmission when appropriate
  • Community members may request removal of culturally inappropriate content

Questions about Guidelines?

If you have questions about these guidelines or need clarification on any policy, please don't hesitate to contact us.
